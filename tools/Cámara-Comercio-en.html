Spanish Chamber of Commerce Tutorial - Foreign Trade Section

     

Services and programs to support the internationalization of Spanish companies

   
   
     

1. What is this section?

     
       

The Spanish Chamber of Commerce offers a section dedicated to Foreign Trade with the aim of supporting companies that:

       
             
  • Wish to start their export process
    •          
  • Seek to diversify international markets
    •          
  • Require support and advisory services for internationalization
    •        
       
          Note: Works closely with territorial Chambers, ICEX, and other public and private institutions.        
     
   
   
     

2. Access to the platform

     
       
             
  1. Visit: camara.es/comercio-exterior
    2.          
  2. Explore the main menu or scroll down to the content organized by programs and services
    3.        
     
   
   
     

3. Featured programs

     
       
             
  • Xpande: Personalized advice for internationalization (with ERDF support)
    •          
  • Xpande Digital: International digital positioning through online marketing
    •          
  • InnoXport: Support for innovative companies that want to go abroad
    •          
  • International Promotion Plan (PIP): Participation in fairs, missions, and commercial actions
    •        
       
          Tip: Check with your local Chamber for specific calls and available aid.        
     
   
   
     

4. Services offered

     
       
             
  • Export potential diagnosis
    •          
  • Specialized training in international trade
    •          
  • B2B meeting agenda and partner search
    •          
  • Advice on regulations, documentation, and barriers
    •        
       

Some services are free and others are co-financed by European funds.

     
   
   
     

5. Practical example

     
       

Example: Participating in the Xpande Digital program

       
             
  1. Enter the “Xpande Digital” section
    2.          
  2. Read the requirements: be an SME, be legally constituted, and have an exportable product
    3.          
  3. Contact your local Chamber and submit an application
    4.          
  4. An international digital marketing plan adapted to your company is developed
    5.        
       

The program can cover up to 80% of eligible costs.

     
   
   
     

6. Recommendations

     
       
             
  • Check the calendar of events and fairs of your nearest Chamber
    •          
  • Request a free diagnosis to identify potential markets
    •          
  • Complement these programs with resources from ICEX, Junta CyL, or Access2Markets
    •
